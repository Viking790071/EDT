Opened from the My business processes list or from the business processes window (for example, Jobs) by clicking Route map.

The procedure of business process execution can be visually represented as a flowchart. The flowchart presents internal logic and lifecycle of a business process as a sequence of route points. Each route point represents a business process stage usually consisting of a single operation. Whenever a business process reaches a route point that requires action by a specific performer, a task is created. Once the performer marks the task as completed, the business process automatically proceeds to the next route point. In this manner, the completion of business process tasks progresses to completion.

When working with the application, the current flowchart status can be displayed for a business process, including both completed and active route points. Completed route points are marked by hatch.

At each route point, one or more actions are available that change the business process status.

Action Who executes Executing the command
Accept for execution The business process performer specified as a performer for the first point of the route.

The performer can accept for execution the task assigned to this role. Thus, the task is hidden from the lists of tasks of other performers of this role.

Create on the basis

An employee who has access to work with a business process.

This command allows you to create a subordinate business process.

Completed

The task assignee (the assignee at the first route point Complete).

After clicking Completed, a task changes its status, goes to the list of completed tasks, and becomes inactive in the lists.

The job supervisor specified as a performer for the second point of the route Check.

The job supervisor confirms the execution by clicking Completed. You can write a comment about the job execution. After that, the task becomes inactive in the lists.

Canceled

The task assignee (the assignee at the first route point Complete).

Clicking the Canceled button means the task is not completed. The task gets the matching status.

The job supervisor specified as a performer for the second point of the route Check.

Supervisor confirms the failure to complete tasks.

Returned

The job supervisor as a performer for the second point of the route Check.

The supervisor can return the task for better execution by the performer by clicking Returned to performer.

Forward

Performer and supervisor

When clicking Forward, the task goes to the task list of another performer.

View tasks by business process route point

  • To view the task list by business process route point, click Show route point tasks. You can also double-click the route point.
